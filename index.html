Before You Start — Read This

A custom build is a collaboration. Not a hand-off.

This is not a situation where you hire me, disappear for three months, and I show up with finished bots. That does not work. The bots we build are built around your firm, your voice, your processes, and your documents. That means you have real involvement throughout — gathering materials, sitting with me on Zoom to review what a bot is doing, recording a training so I can hear how you explain something to your staff, telling me when something is off.

I lead the build. I tell you exactly what I need and when. But you will have homework. The firms that get the best results are the ones that show up to that homework. Come into this knowing that, and we will build something genuinely useful.

04

How You Work Today

Most people cannot answer the question "what are your pain points?" — and that is fine. So I am not going to ask you that. Instead, walk me through what actually happens in your firm. Answer what you know and skip what you don't. Real details are more useful than polished answers.

From the first call to the signed retainer — When a potential new client contacts your firm, who answers? What do they ask? Where does that information go? How does the client end up with a signed agreement, and how long does the whole process usually take?
Once a client is hired — Pick a case that is open on your desk right now. What does your staff do on that file this week? How do they know what needs to be done? Where do documents live? What's the most time-consuming task that does not actually require a law degree to do?
The one that keeps coming back — What task does your staff do over and over again that could theoretically be done the same way every single time? What do they have to look up, copy, or recreate that they have already done a hundred times before?
What falls through the cracks — What is the thing that gets dropped, delayed, or forgotten most often? Not the dramatic failures — the quiet ones. The follow-up that did not happen. The document that got filed late. The client who waited too long to hear back.
